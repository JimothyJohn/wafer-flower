# Halo v3 — adhesive mount, zero frame hardware

## What's on the frame (per segment, x9)
Dovetails, three 0.5 mm bond-line nubs, an underside keyhole. Nothing else.
The bonding land is generated by cutting the segment blank with the
wafer-back plane, so it is coplanar with the wafer underside by construction
and covers every point where the wafer footprint overlaps the frame
(~110 x 40 mm lens through the segment middle at default params).
Want more land? Widen body_ri/body_ro — do NOT expect land at the segment
ends; the wafer has curved away there.

## Bonding procedure (bench, one segment at a time)
1. Print jig once. Fit-check jig on a bare segment: dovetail seats, rails
   straddle the base with no rock.
2. Dry-run: wafer onto the land, slide until edge rests on the downhill
   fence, finger-press toward the 180° fence pad until it touches both.
   That's centered. Practice this before adhesive.
3. Mask nothing; apply MS-polymer (SikaFlex 252 / Loctite PL) in a
   serpentine bead over the land, staying ~5 mm inside its edge.
4. Place wafer, center against fences as rehearsed, install clamp knob,
   turn until the pad JUST contacts, then 1/4 turn. The nubs stop it —
   you are setting bond line, not applying force.
5. Cure per datasheet (MS polymers: handle 24 h, full 72 h). Remove jig,
   next segment.
6. After all nine: dovetail the ring together, hang on 3 keyholes.

## Adhesive
MS-polymer or E6000, not rigid epoxy: PLA/PETG vs silicon CTE mismatch over
a 110 mm bond puts thermal shear into the wafer. If you insist on epoxy,
use a flexibilized one (3M DP125) and keep the 0.5 mm bond line.

## Known v1 risks — check on first render/print
1. UNRENDERED (no OpenSCAD in my environment). projection() of the tilted
   cylinder is the one construct most likely to be slow or need a render
   (F6) instead of preview; if it drags, replace wafer_footprint_prism()
   with a linear_extrude of an approximated ellipse (semi-axes 150 radial,
   150*cos(tilt) tangential, centered at R_pitch).
2. Jig fence struts and bridge are placed by hand-calculated offsets —
   expect to nudge them after first F5. Printables (segment, jig) before
   scene modes.
3. The downhill fence assumes bonding with the segment flat on the bench
   (wafer plane tilted 20 deg, low side = local -Y/270 deg). If you bond in
   any other orientation, move the fence to whatever side is downhill.
4. Land surface is a plane cut across layer lines — print segments land-up
   with fine layers, or hit the land with one pass of filler/sand for full
   adhesive contact. Nubs survive light sanding; don't flatten them.
