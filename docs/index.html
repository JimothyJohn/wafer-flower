OP 010

CAD — one segment, 6 features

  1. Variables: #R, #theta, #Ri, #bw, #tmin, #bond from the calculator above once it looks right.
  2. Own wafer plane: through the radial line at (#R, 0), angled #theta from Top. Offset a copy by −(0.3875 + #bond) → the land plane.
  3. Neighbor plane: the own wafer plane, revolved −40° about center (model it directly: radial line at 40°, same angle construction). Offset a copy by −3 mm → the clearance plane.
  4. Blank: sector 40° × Ri→Ri+bw, extruded tall (60). Flat bottom at z = −(y_max·tanθ + #bond + #tmin) where y_max = (Ri+bw)·sin20° — this produces the taper with your minimum thickness at the trailing side.
  5. Two cuts: remove everything above the land plane, then everything above the clearance plane. Cut order doesn't matter; the surviving top is bondable only where the land plane governs.
  6. Dovetails on the radial end faces (12/16 × 8, female +0.25) and you're done. No other features this iteration.
Design change vs Rev A
The neighbor clearance cut (step 3/5) is new and mandatory. Without it, each segment's land near its leading joint rises ~2·ρ·sin20°·tanθ above the adjacent wafer's plane and collides with it (34 mm of interference at θ=10°, ρ=280). The cut costs a ~6° corner of land; ~34° of bondable arc remains.

Why T3 is the gate
Gravity shear on the bond is ~0.6 kPa against a ~500 kPa capability — the load case that matters is the 10–30 °C swing driving ~80 µm of differential CTE slip at the bond ends. A compliant bond (foam tape or SMP) absorbs that as a few % shear strain; a rigid bond puts it into the silicon. T3 with real thermal cycles is the only test that proves your specific adhesive + your specific print surface. Full-surface adhesive coverage adds nothing: area was never the limit, and longer bonds see more end slip.
OP 020

Bond — bench, one station at a time

  1. IPA-wipe land and wafer contact zone; let flash.
  2. Tape path: two 20–25 mm strips arced on the land, ≥5 mm inside its boundary, roller-pressed. Liners off only with wafer in hand. Liquid path (SMP): serpentine bead, 5 mm inside the land edge; Ø4 × #bond printed nubs (3×) set the bondline.
  3. Rest wafer edge on the jig's downhill fence first, lower like closing a book. Confirm both fences touch before full contact — tape gives one attempt.
  4. Press over the land through a foam pad (tape: 15 s firm; SMP: clamp to nub contact + ¼ turn, 24 h in jig).
  5. Repeat ×9, then join the ring.
