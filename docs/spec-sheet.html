Read this first

The silicon is not the fragile part of this design — the joint is, and the exposed wafer face is. Every adhesive and gravity load in this assembly runs at 47× to 4000× margin. Two things do not: the printed dovetail at the ring joints (2.0× on a single-point hang), and the unsupported wafer area, where a 1.5 N push — about 150 g, a firm fingertip — reaches the fracture allowable.

Handle it by the frame. Hang it from two points. Do not hang anything from it.

Why the bond is not the concern

The adhesive could hold roughly a hundred times the wafer's weight indefinitely. Gravity was never the design load — differential thermal expansion is. See §5.

Known limitation — ring joint

In the single-dovetail configuration hung from one point, the joint runs at 2.0× against a printed-PETG allowable, in the print's weak direction, under permanent load. PETG creeps. Every other improvement to this design — a larger wafer, more tilt, moving the bond band outboard — makes this number worse.

Mitigate by any one of: two dovetails per joint face (restores most of the band depth, ~10× the section modulus); two hang points at ±50° from top (drops the moment ~4×); or resting the ring on a ledge rather than hanging it, which puts the ring in compression and drives the moment to near zero.

§ 6

Handling

  • DoCarry the assembly by the printed frame only, supported from below at two or more segments.
  • DoHandle loose wafers by the edge, fingers at the rim, wafer vertical.
  • DoPress only over the bond land — the region where the frame backs the wafer. Everywhere else the wafer is a 0.775 mm unsupported plate.
  • NeverTouch, lean on, or rest anything against the exposed wafer face. A 150 g fingertip push at the outer edge reaches fracture.
  • NeverFlex a wafer. Silicon gives no warning — no yield, no bend, no crack. It goes from intact to shattered.
  • NeverHang, clip, drape or rest anything on the halo. Rated mass is its own, with zero headroom.
  • NeverTransport assembled. Separate at the dovetails and pack segments individually, wafer-side up, interleaved with foam.
  • CareWafer shards are razor-sharp and near-invisible. If one breaks, treat it as broken glass: gloves, stiff card to sweep, tape to lift fines, then vacuum.
§ 7

Mounting

  • DoUse two anchors at ±50° from top, each rated ≥ 95 N (4× the 23.0 N assembly weight). This is the single cheapest structural improvement available.
  • DoAnchor into a stud where possible, or a rated hollow-wall anchor. Adhesive wall hooks are not acceptable.
  • DoAllow 60 mm minimum clearance in front of the wall; the wafers swing ±26 mm off the ring plane at θ = 10°.
  • CareThe assembly centre of mass sits 6.5 mm off the wall plane, producing a 0.14 N·m tip-off moment. Anchors must resist pull-out, not just shear.
  • AvoidWalls shared with laundry appliances, garage door motors, or speakers. The wafer overhang resonates at 24 Hz, squarely inside that excitation band.
  • AvoidDirect sunlight, exterior walls prone to condensation, bathrooms, and unconditioned garages or lofts.
  • AvoidMounting above seating, beds, or walkways. This is a brittle assembly at 2.35 kg.
§ 8

Cleaning & maintenance

  • DoDust with dry compressed air at low pressure, held at an angle, from the frame side outward. This is the only no-contact method and should be the default.
  • DoFor fingerprints, dampen a microfibre with 91 % IPA and wipe only over the supported land area, with the segment removed and lying flat.
  • NeverSpray any liquid at the assembly. It wicks along the wafer underside into the bondline and lifts the adhesive.
  • NeverLet acetone touch the printed frame — it crazes PETG and softens PLA. It is harmless on silicon, which is exactly how accidents happen.
  • NeverUse paper towel, abrasive cloth, or glass cleaner on the polished wafer face. The mirror finish scratches permanently and the ammonia attacks the print.
  • AnnualInspect per §9. Check anchor torque and dovetail seating; both are quick and both are load-bearing.

Notes

Basis of ratings

1 Silicon has no single fracture strength; it is flaw-limited and scatters widely. 30 MPa is a conservative design allowable for a handled wafer with a bevelled but unpolished edge. Point-load stress is computed as σ = 6PL/(befft²) with an effective width of 0.5–1.0 L, giving 1.5–3.0 N at the free edge; the lower bound is quoted.

2 At a 2× design factor on printed PETG, the allowable assembly mass through a single centred dovetail is 2.34 kg against an actual, slicer-measured 2.35 kg. A single-point hang no longer meets a 2× factor at all — hang from two points (±50°), which restores ~4× of moment headroom.

3 Bond shear strain γ = Δα·ΔT·Lmax/tb, with Δα ≈ 57 ppm/K and Lmax = 96 mm from the land centroid. Because Lmax and not area sets the stress, a larger bond patch is a worse bond patch — adhesive is applied as two short arcs near the land centroid, never full coverage.

4 First bending mode of the unsupported outboard wafer, modelled as a uniform cantilever of length L with E = 130 GPa.

All values are derived from a parametric solver, cross-checked against an independent implementation with all outputs matching. They are analysis, not measurement — except the printed masses, which are measured from sliced G-code (OrcaSlicer, 0.20 mm, 45 % infill) and carried through the load numbers. Test gates T1–T7 — neighbour clearance, dovetail fit, adhesive shear with 10–30 °C cycling, land flatness, taper minimum, centring repeatability and the dovetail hang test — must pass before any of this is treated as verified.
